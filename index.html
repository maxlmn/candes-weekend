Getting there

Château de Candes — Art & Spa
46 route de Compostelle, 37500 Candes-Saint-Martin

Halfway between Angers and Tours, in one of the "Plus Beaux Villages de France", where the Loire meets the Vienne. Nearest stations: Saumur or Chinon. Public parking sits just by Maison Rempart and Maison Perrière.

More on the estate: chateaudecandes.com

Nearest station: Saumur (15 min by car), with direct trains from Angers. Also Port-Boulet (17 min), or Angers and Tours (about 1 hr each).

Two spas — the Zen Garden with heated pools and sauna, and Spa Côté Loire (by reservation). Bring swimwear.

A Little Guide

Around the Village

three places worth your boots within a few minutes of the château

Candes-Saint-Martin

Right at our doorstep

Our own village — one of the official Plus Beaux Villages de France, set where the Loire meets the Vienne. Wander the lower lane where the old Loire boatmen lived, find the troglodyte dwellings in the cliff, and step inside the Gothic Collégiale Saint-Martin.

On foot · just below the château

Montsoreau

The next village over

A Renaissance château built right in the riverbed of the Loire — the only one of its kind — now a striking contemporary-art museum (the world's largest Art & Language collection). The rooftop terraces give a 360° view over the river and vineyards. The village is itself a Plus Beau Village.

A few minutes · short walk or drive along the river

Fontevraud Abbey

A short drive inland

One of Europe's great medieval abbeys and the royal resting place of the Plantagenets — Henry II, Eleanor of Aquitaine and Richard the Lionheart all lie here beneath painted effigies. Don't miss the extraordinary Romanesque kitchen and the vast cloisters.

~15 min · by car, inland from the river

Château de Brézé

A short drive south

A Renaissance château hiding an astonishing secret: beneath it lies a whole troglodyte fortress — over a kilometre of carved galleries, kitchens, cellars and an underground bakery — ringed by the deepest dry moat in Europe (18 m). The estate still makes its own Saumur wine.

~20 min · by car, vineyards en route

Happening This Weekend

Anjou Vélo Vintage

26–28 June 2026 · the very weekend we're here

🚲 Retro bikes take over Saumur

By happy coincidence, Europe's largest vintage cycling festival fills nearby Saumur while we're at the château. Expect period bikes, swing bands, a free vintage village in the old town, and local fouées and Saumur wines everywhere — all in glorious retro dress (polka dots, braces, berets).

The signed rides (30–65 km, registration required) roll through the Saumurois vineyards on Saturday and Sunday; the vintage village in central Saumur is free and open all weekend if you just fancy a wander. A fun, optional detour about 20 minutes from us.

More at anjou-velo-vintage.com

A Glass or Two

Saumur & Its Vineyards

we're in the heart of Loire wine country — here's the lay of the land

The vines around us grow on tuffeau, the soft white limestone the châteaux are also carved from. Two grapes rule: Cabernet Franc (the local "Breton") for reds, and Chenin Blanc for whites and the famous sparklers — many aged in cellars dug straight into the rock.

Saumur-Champigny

Cabernet Franc · red

The star red of the area, from eight villages just upriver — including Montsoreau next door. Fresh, chalky and floral in the classic style; deeper and age-worthy from the newer growers.

Saumur Blanc

Chenin Blanc · white

Dry, mineral whites off the same limestone — bright and food-friendly, with a fine chalky backbone.

Saumur Fines Bulles & Crémant de Loire

Chenin · sparkling

The region's calling card. The big sparkling houses (Bouvet-Ladubay, Ackerman, Langlois) sit just west of Saumur, their bottles ageing in tuffeau caves.

Chinon · Bourgueil · St-Nicolas

Cabernet Franc · red

Just across the Vienne and the Loire, 20–30 min away — more excellent Cabernet Franc country, perfect for a tasting detour.

A few estates worth seeking out

All in the Saumur area: Clos Rougeard, Sylvain Dittière, Théo Blet, Guiberteau, Domaine du Collier and Château Yvonne.

Some will welcome very small groups for a visit, but only by arrangement — please get in touch with them well in advance.
